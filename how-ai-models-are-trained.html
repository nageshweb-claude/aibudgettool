How AI Models Are Actually Trained

June 2026 · 4 min read

Training an LLM involves pre-training on raw text, then fine-tuning with human feedback to align it with user needs.

Stage 1: Pre-training

The model reads an enormous amount of text and learns to predict the next word. This stage gives it broad language and world knowledge but no sense of how to be a helpful assistant.

Stage 2: Supervised Fine-Tuning

Human-written example conversations teach the model the format of helpful, instruction-following responses.

Stage 3: RLHF / Constitutional AI

Human feedback or principle-based self-critique refines the model further, making it more helpful, honest, and safe.

Stage 4: Evaluation and Release

Extensive testing for safety, accuracy, and capability before the model becomes available to the public.
